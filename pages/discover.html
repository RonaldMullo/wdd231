Our History

Founded on the ruins of an ancient indigenous city by Sebastián de Benalcázar on December 6, 1534, Quito, officially San Francisco de Quito, stands as one of the oldest capitals in South America. Its historic center, one of the best preserved on the continent, is a testament to its glorious past as the main artistic and cultural hub of the Royal Audience of Quito during the colonial era, which earned it the declaration as the First Cultural Heritage of Humanity by UNESCO in 1978. It was also the cradle of independence with the First Cry of Independence on August 10, 1809, and today it is the vibrant political and cultural capital of the Republic of Ecuador.

The Quito Chamber of Commerce (CCQ) was founded on January 28, 1889, by a visionary group of merchants and entrepreneurs, with the goal of promoting economic development, representing the interests of the productive sector, and fostering order and progress in commerce in the capital. Throughout its more than a century of history, it has established itself as one of the most important guild institutions in the country, adapting to changes and expanding its services to include professional training (with its renowned Business School), certification of commercial documents, organization of fairs, and generation of economic studies that are a reference for decision-making in the sector.
